Membership Events:

'Carshalton Beeches Chapter'

Combining peer-to-peer conversation with wandering the local area, our 'Carshalton Beeches Chapter' unites local men, meeting weekly, on a Wednesday, outside Carshalton Beeches Station, which is convenient and easily accessible, being served by good transport links and unrestricted parking. Typically lasting up-to 2hrs a session, including group refreshment and walking no more than 3 miles, we welcome men from all abilities and ages, walking at a gentle pace.

'Croydon Chapter'

Combining peer-to-peer conversation with exploring a variety of leisure activities, including archery and riffle shooting in the local woodlands, our 'Croydon Chapter' unites local men, meeting weekly, on a Thursday, at Pinewood Activity Centre, which is convenient and easily accessible, being served by good transport links and unrestricted parking. Typically lasting up-to 2hrs a session, including group refreshment and walking no more than 3 miles, we welcome men from all abilities and ages.

'High Miller' Walks:

Our 'High Miler Chapter' is another opportunity to get walking, while enjoying conversation with other men, from a mixture of our local groups. While open to all, this is a longer walk than our local sessions, but still enjoying a gentle pace and group refreshment. We typically walk over 5 miles in a session, lasting up-to 3hrs, on a weekend. We typically meet weekly, on a Sunday morning, with the location changing each time, to sample different areas of outstanding beauty and historical value.

'BOB FIT'

The 'Band of Brothers' Fit Club is a great opportunity to get fit and enjoy the outside space, while enjoying conversation with other local men. While open to all, this is a fitness group meeting weekly, typically for a 1hr period, enjoying physical exercise for up-to 3 miles at a time.

'Brothers Over Breakfast' sessions:

We recognise the importance for socialising, as well as physical exercise and task building, which is why we've developed our 'Brothers Over Breakfast' sessions. Typically meeting on a monthly basis, at different local venues, we enjoy a breakfast¹ and chat, offering peer-to-peer support.

¹ - While we are working hard to attract discounts, unfortunately the cost of food and drinks are not covered.

'Building Brothers' Workshops:

Our 'Building Brothers' workshops provide training and support to individuals, while encouraging conversation. Typically, these sessions last a couple of hours, meeting on a weekly basis, where we enjoy woodwork and carpentry tasks, by building something for the local community to enjoy.

Drop-In Sessions:

If physical activity isn't your interest, we also host weekly 'Drop-in sessions' in Community Spaces, to allow men the chance to talk within a supportive community, with refreshments and games on offer too.

'Gents Get Gardening' sessions:

Join us for something different, getting men talking over gardening, to help increase the feeling of positivity, by working on a project and feeling a sense of satisfaction in taking care of a local treasure, within an area. Out 'Gents Get Gardening' sessions typically meet weekly, for an hour or so.
